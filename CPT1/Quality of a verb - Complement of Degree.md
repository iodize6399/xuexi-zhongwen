# Structures

## Complement of Degree

`S + V + 得 + Adj.`

Simple structure that defines the quality of the action.

>[!info] Negation
>
>`S + V + 得 + 不 + Adj.`
> 
>不 after 得

## Verbs with both an object and a complement of degree

`S + V + O + V + 得 + Adj.`

Use when comparing or stressing the objective.

>[!important] Duplication of verb
>Notice how the verb is duplicated.

> 他生词念得很好，课文念得不太熟。
> He reads new words well, but he doesn't read the text too fluently.

## Preposed object

`O + S + V + 得 + Adj.`

- Use when the structure is complicated.
- object is put before the subject or verb if object is to be stressed or when it is complicated

### when object is stressed

>他生词念得==很==好，课文念得==不太==好。
>He reads new words clearly, (but) he reads text not well.

### when object is complicated

>==老师的问题他回答==得很对。
>He answers teacher's question correctly.

## Patterns

For duplicate verb structure, add adverb just **before the duplicated verb**

> ...翻译句子==也==翻译得很好. 
> (He) also translates sentences very well.

For non-duplicate structure, add adverb **before the adjective and immediately after 得**

> ...排球打得==也==很不错。
> (He) also plays volleyball not bad.

## Unique sentences

> 他们玩儿得很==高兴==。
> They have a great time.
> They play very happily.

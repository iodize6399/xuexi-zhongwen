# Some fleeting notes

- There are total `21` initials and `38` finals
- Beijing dialect has `4` basic tones
- a syllable pronounced in different tones will have different meanings
- Chinese characters are written symbols of the Chinese language
- Usually, one character stands for one syllable
- certain syllable are weak and short, called the neutral or 5th tone

# 楼

Suffix 楼 to convert a place into building, e.g., 教学楼，办公楼，工厂楼，邮局楼，宿舍楼

# 是的

when one agrees with one another, reply with `是的` or `是`

# 男 and 女

`男` and `女` cannot stand alone.

>男朋友/女朋友
>Boyfriend/Girlfriend
>男医生/女医生
>Male doctor/Female doctor


# Measure word for 本子 - Notebook

Use `个` NOT `本`

>他有几个本子？
>How many notebooks does he have?

# Written language vs spoken language

## 语 vs 文

`语` is usually suffixed for spoken language after the country's name
`文` is suffix for written language

>[!tip]
>Use `文` when the object is "written" in nature.
>>中文书，中文杂志

`汉语` is the spoken Chinese vs `中文` is written Chinese
`法语` is the spoken French vs `法文` is the written French
`德语` is the spoken German vs `德文` is the written German

# Plural suffix 们

- Suffix 们 is used after pronoun or noun.
- indicates plurality
- A noun can indicate either singular or plural, if context makes it clear that it is plural then 们 is not used
